Books by Sonia Nimr

Novels

Thunderbird 1

(طائر الرعد 1)

ISBN: 9789950260856

Age: young adults

Reviewer Link

The Thunderbird trilogy is a fast-paced, time-traveling fantasy adventure centered on Noor, a young orphaned Palestinian girl. Beginning in the present, Noor must travel back in time to collect four magical feathers from the phoenix before it burns and save the world. With the aid of a djinn taking the form of a cat and girls who look identical to Noor—each possessing one of the bird’s powers—this initial volume launches Noor on her journey through different historical periods, striving to keep the wall between worlds intact.

Thunderbird 2

(طائر الرعد 2)

ISBN: 9789950270350

Age: young adults

Reviewer Link

In this second volume, Noor finds herself in the year 1177 in Jerusalem during the Crusades. With the help of the Djinn and Zainab—a girl who has the power of invisibility—and her local resistance group, Noor must again find a feather belonging to the thunderbird before it burns, to prevent the world of the djinn and other creatures from collapsing into the human world. Along the way, Noor assists the resistance in saving hundreds of books and manuscripts from destruction. Like the first book in the trilogy, Book Two combines magic with real events from Palestinian history, taking the reader on a wild and adventurous ride through time.

Thunderbird 3

(طائر الرعد 3)

ISBN: 9789950270473

Age: young adults

Reviewer Link

In the third and final book of the Thunderbird trilogy, Noor finds herself in Byzantine Jerusalem in the year 638 CE, where the city is under siege. Joined by Sabeeka, the djinn who takes the form of a cat, Noor meets the twins Helena and Julian, who possess the power of healing. Together, they team up to find the thunderbird. However, before they can accomplish this, they are swallowed by a giant, worm-like creature which transports them to the world beyond the wall separating the djinn world from the human realm. In the terrifying, dark, gloomy Bacosala’a world of worm–like creatures, Noor and her friends are rescued and delivered to the colorful and peaceful world of the Sakarik. Here, they discover that Bacosala’a are stealing the golden liquid that sustains the Sakarik's life. Will Noor and her friends rescue the Sakarik and return to the human world in time to find the last two thunderbird feathers needed to save the world?

Wondrous Journeys in Strange Lands

ISBN: 9789950260108

Age: young adults

Reviewer Link

In a tent at the foot of a mountain in Palestine, hundreds of years ago, our storyteller and her twin sister were born. Her name is Qamar (Moon), and her sister is Shams (Sun). Their small caravan is journeying from their mother’s city back to their father’s remote ancestral village atop the mountain. This village suffers from isolation—and a curse—which their young family tries to undo. But when both parents' lives are cut short, Qamar and her sister are left orphans. Qamar decides to pursue her parents’ dreams of discovering the world—its people, places, and stories. With the red book that brought her parents together in hand, she sets out on a daring journey across empires, traveling by caravans and ships. Telling tales to survive, Qamar crosses deserts and seas: to Jerusalem and Gaza, Egypt, Tangier, Andalusia, and Genoa; Abyssinia, India, the Maldives, and Yemen. She faces kidnappers, is sold as a slave to the house of a mad king, studies with a polymath, disguises herself as a man, and falls in love for the first time—with a pirate. Qamar searches irrepressibly for life, uncovering endless stories within stories.

Mandoura (مندورة)

ISBN: 978-965-7439-17-3

Age: Children

Mandoura is a curious girl with a heart full of wonder. She’s captivated by the world around her, always asking questions. Her insatiable curiosity leads her to a tiny world inhabited by the rainbow painters. But when they discover they’ve run out of red, Mandoura embarks on a magical adventure, learning profound lessons about nature along the way. Will she be able to help them find the color red and finish the rainbow?

The Lion Who Lost His Sleep (الأسد الذي فقد نومه)

ISBN: 1407-193093

Age: Children

Reviewer Link

This is the tale of a lion, the king of the forest, who loves nothing more than to sleep. While danger looms from fearsome machines with teeth, the animals turn to him for help. The lion promises to solve their problems and devise a clever plan, but instead, he dozes off again and again, pretending to work while truly lost in slumber. Will the lion wake up in time to save his kingdom, or will his laziness lead to disaster?

Zalloutah (زلّوطة)

ISBN: 9789950260344

Age: Children

Reviewer Link

This is the tale of a fly who loves her cozy home in the rubbish bin. When she gets into a squabble with other flies over her favorite spot, things take a turn for the worse. Ultimately, the landlady decides to close the bin, forcing her to leave. Now, she must find a new place to call home!

قصة أولها خيال واخرها خيال

ISBN: 9789950260535

Age: Children

The king wanted his only son and heir to get married and presented him with choices: a princess, a rich girl, or a beautiful girl. However, the prince had a different idea; he wanted a girl who reads books and knows how to tell stories. He sought a story that begins and ends with lies. Girls came from all corners of the kingdom, each trying but failing to tell the prince a story that began and ended with lies. Just as the prince’s patience began to wane, an ordinary girl in a simple dress approached him. She began to tell a most imaginative and fantastical story about a giant rooster that emerged from a round egg , a town inside a water melon and being shot in the sky by a canon and other fantastical details.
